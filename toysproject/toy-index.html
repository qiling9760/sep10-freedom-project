What are Toys and Puzzles?

Welcome to our website, in this website we will briefly provide information about Puzzles and Toys. The history of toys and puzzles can be traced back to prehistoric times. They come in many different forms such as wood and plastic. As technology penetrates deeper into our lives, it also impacts toys and puzzles. Toys and puzzles become more delicate and advanced as technology is involved in them. This website will look at how technology has changed toys and puzzles and how it will further impact them.

Present Technologies

As technology becomes more advanced, people can now play puzzles on their devices. There are applications that help the users to make their toys more fun. The toys can also be used for educational purposes. Smart toys are toys that involve technologies and help with learning, creativity, and communication with kids.

Future technologies

  • Create your own toys by imagination

    • There will be a headset and a machine
    • When the user puts on the headset, they can control the machine using their mind
    • The user will put materials that are used for making the toy in the machine and imagine the toy’s outlook and properties, the machine will then start making the toy
    • If the user wants to repair an old toy, the headset will read the user’s memory and the user can decide what period of their life that they want the toy to go back to

  • Make your favorite character come alive

    • In the future, there will be an AI that can act like your favorite character in a book/movie/game, etc and talk to you
    • The AI will collect the character’s information such as their personalities, background, tone of voice, and etc
    • You can create your own character and make them come alive too, but you have to give the AI enough information for it to understand what kind of person it is going to act
    • You can make yourself relate to your favorite character. For example, make yourself become your favorite character’s lover/family/opponent and create fun between you and them

  • Turn your life into a puzzle game

    • A smart glasses
    • When the user puts on the smart glasses, the glasses will generate challenges based on the user’s environment
    • For example, when the user is running to a subway station, the glasses will make it become a running game and generate coins on the way and set the destination to be the subway station. The user will need to collect the coins and get to the subway station on time.
    • The user can choose to make what they have gone through when using the smart glasses into a game
    • Since different people have different lives, there will be many different puzzle games based on real-life experiences and the users can try out other’s experiences

  • A toy that can make music based on the user’s feeling

    • A bracelet and a toy
    • The bracelet can detect the user’s feelings and transfer the date to the toy. The toy can make a song that matches the user’s feelings (won’t be played out loud, if you have a headphone it will be played in your headphone)
    • For example, when the user’s heart beats fast because they have a crush on someone, the toy will make a romantic song

Brickit

Brickit is an app that can help its users build a product out of old legos. The user will have to pile their old legos and use Brickit to scan them. The app will identify the legos and give the user ideas about what they can build with the old legos.

Jigsaw Puzzles

A jigsaw puzzle is a type of puzzle that interlocks the puzzle which involves piecing together and connecting smaller pieces to form one large form of picture. In the early years, people would buy puzzles from the store to play, but now that technology has evolved over time, there are puzzle games that allow people to play them online.

Sudoku

Sudoku is an individual game that can be played on paper or any of your electronics, when you play Sudoku you are to start with the 3x3 that must be filled from 1-9, then work your way down, until you finish with your 9x9 grid.

Unpacking

Unpacking is a puzzle game where you organize the person’s room, and your objective is to make the person's new home neat, thinking about where to put it based on the things you have and your preference on how you want to be.

Monument Valley

Monument Valley is a puzzle game developed by Ustwo Games. In the game, players will solve mazes and guide the main character, Princess Ida, to her destination. In the mazes, there are obstacles that stop Princess Ida from moving, and the players can start rearranging pieces to form a path for Princess Ida to go through.

Hatchimals

Hatchimals are plush toys that have sensors and microphones that allow them to communicate with kids. They can play music and games with kids when kids interact with them. By taking care of Hatchimals, kids can learn to love and treat animals with patience.

SGILE RC robot

SGILE RC robot is a robotic toy that can walk, dance, and play music. It utilizes gesture censoring technology and can be controlled by hand motions. Robotics can teach kids problem-solving skills and expand their creativity. It also gets them introduced to programming.

3D printing

A process that creates three-dimensional objects using a digital design

Toybox is an application that many people use to 3D print toys. It is appropriate for kids aged 6+. The users can import models from the internet or create their own design on ThinkerCad. After they select their design, they can use the ToyBox 3D printer to print it out. 3D printing toys can help to build STEM skills and experiences. It allows children to turn their imagination into a real project.
